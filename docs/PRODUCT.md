# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is academic: future PhD supervisors, research collaborators, fellowship or grant evaluators, academic recruiters, conference contacts, and technical peers. Secondary readers include industry researchers and advanced students.

Visitors should be able to understand Islam I. Abdulaal's current research identity quickly, then move into publications, research themes, selected projects, software artifacts, talks, or CV material without encountering promotional clutter.

## Product Purpose

This website is a long-lived academic research profile for integrated photonics. It presents validated work, current research direction, citable outputs, and technical artifacts in a way that can grow from early-career researcher to PhD researcher and later independent scientist without requiring a new identity system.

Success means a technically serious visitor leaves with the impression that the research trajectory is coherent, grounded, and worth following.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, research-first.

The site should feel like a maintained scientific notebook and a lightweight lab-style profile. It should communicate rigor, focus, and technical maturity through the structure and evidence of the work rather than through assertive personal branding language.

## Anti-references

Avoid startup landing pages, tech influencer portfolios, AI company websites, productivity pages, over-designed SaaS templates, glassmorphism, neon visual language, excessive animation, hero-metric layouts, broad achievement stacking, founder language, futurist language, and exaggerated claims.

Avoid sections or labels such as Ventures, Startup, Ecosystem, Mission, Vision, Services, Mentorship, Personal Philosophy, and generic Blog.

Avoid phrases such as incoming, future, aspiring, visionary, innovator, founder, mission-driven, changing the world, building the future, next generation, and revolutionary unless they appear inside a cited work title.

## Design Principles

1. Coherence over breadth: fewer sections and tools, each tied clearly to integrated photonics.
2. Evidence over impression management: publications, methods, artifacts, and technical descriptions carry the signal.
3. Methods as tools: AI, PINNs, and ML appear as computational methods within photonics, not as the core identity.
4. Academic durability: copy, URLs, typography, and layout should age well across MSc, PhD, and research-scientist stages.
5. Quiet confidence: the interface should be understated, readable, and technically serious.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, semantic HTML, keyboard-accessible navigation, visible focus states, reduced-motion support, readable line lengths, descriptive link text, and accessible PDF alternatives where available.
